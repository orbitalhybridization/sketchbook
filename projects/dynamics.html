Dynamical Systems and Human Interaction

Introduction

I've always been interested in human interaction and communication. As a teen, I liked trying to express my inner thoughts via non-linguistic expressions that I called "inclinations." The idea was that these intuitions were more efficient than words, and that they could be used to communicate complex ideas in a more direct way.

Scientific Foundations

The basic scientific premise that got me into this is the idea of modeling human interaction as a dynamical system. This started while I was taking classes on dynamical systems and human interaction, and I started to wonder how we could use dynamical systems tools to model human interaction.

Influential Research

1. Pedersen & Stevensen (2014)

In their paper, they propose a model for human interaction that spans several spatiotemporal scales, from the microscale of biological processes to the macroscale of phylogeny. This philosophical approach to human interaction was fascinating to me.

2. Dale & Kello (2017)

This paper offers three conditions for "sensemaking" in humans: dynamic memory, multimodal integration, and timescale integration, all properties also shared by reservoir computing models.

3. Dr. Uri Hasson's Research

Dr. Hasson's work demonstrates how the brain processes information during communication and naturalistic stimuli. His findings highlight the importance of temporal receptive windows for creating coherent understanding, especially at the event level.

Future Goals

My goal with this line of research is to improve communication. As I thought from a young age, communication is more than just words, and I believe that by understanding the dynamical systems that underlie human interaction, we can improve communication and understanding between people. This is a lofty goal, but one that I believe is worth pursuing.
